Please act as a Senior Product Manager. Your task is to ingest the provided context and draft a comprehensive Product Requirements Document (PRD). If the document is missing specific details, use your professional judgment to propose "best-practice" solutions, but clearly label them as [Proposed].

PRD Structure & Format:
Executive Summary: High-level overview of the "What" and "Why."

Problem Statement: What specific user pain point are we solving?

Goals & Success Metrics: Define what success looks like (KPIs).

User Personas: Who is this for?

User Stories: Format: "As a [user], I want to [action], so that [value]."

Functional Requirements: A prioritized table of features (P0, P1, P2).

Non-Functional Requirements: Security, scalability, and performance needs.

User Flow & UX Requirements: Describe the logic of the user journey.

Assumptions & Constraints: Technical or business limitations.

Tone: Professional, concise, and technical enough for engineering teams while remaining accessible for stakeholders. Use Markdown for formatting and tables for the requirements section.
